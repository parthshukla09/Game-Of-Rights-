Article 239: Administration of Union Territories

  • Administration of Union Territories:
    • Scope: Article 239 deals with the administration of Union Territories. It provides for the appointment of administrators (Lieutenant Governors or Chief Commissioners) by the President of India to oversee the administration of these territories.
    • Governance Structure: The article outlines the structure and responsibilities of administrators in managing the affairs of Union Territories, ensuring effective governance.
  • Purpose and Impact:
    • Centralized Administration: The provision ensures centralized control and administration of Union Territories, maintaining consistency and oversight in governance.
    • Effective Management: By appointing administrators, the article supports the effective management and administration of Union Territories, addressing their unique needs and challenges.
    • Presidential Authority: The role of the President in appointing administrators reinforces the central authority and oversight over Union Territories.

Article 240: Power to Make Laws for Union Territories

  • Legislative Powers:
    • Scope: Article 240 grants Parliament the power to make laws for Union Territories, including laws related to their administration, governance, and legal matters.
    • Law-Making Authority: Parliament has the authority to legislate on various aspects of Union Territories, including their administrative structures and legal frameworks.
  • Purpose and Impact:
    • Centralized Legislation: The provision ensures that legislative authority over Union Territories is centralized, allowing for consistent and uniform laws across these regions.
    • Administrative Control: By providing Parliament with the power to make laws, the article supports effective governance and management of Union Territories.
    • Legal Framework: The ability to legislate on Union Territories ensures that they have a clear legal framework and administrative structure to address their specific needs and requirements.

Article 241: High Court for Union Territories

  • Establishment and Jurisdiction:
    • Scope: Article 241 provides for the establishment of a High Court specifically for Union Territories if necessary. This article allows for the creation of a High Court to address the legal and judicial needs of Union Territories, ensuring they have access to a higher level of judicial authority.
    • Legislative Authority: The establishment of such a High Court requires parliamentary legislation, which ensures that the creation and functioning of the High Court are carried out with proper legal backing and oversight.
  • Purpose and Impact:
    • Judicial Access: The provision ensures that Union Territories, which are directly governed by the central government, have their own High Courts for efficient handling of legal matters and appeals.
    • Administrative Efficiency: Establishing a High Court in Union Territories helps streamline the judicial process, allowing for better management of legal cases and administrative functions.
    • Legislative Oversight: The requirement for parliamentary legislation ensures that the creation of a High Court in Union Territories is done with legislative scrutiny and aligns with the needs of these territories.

Article 242: Administration of Union Territories with Legislature

  • Governance Framework:
    • Scope: Article 242 pertains to the administration of Union Territories that have their own legislature. This article provides for the establishment of a legislative assembly in Union Territories where the President decides it is appropriate to have one.
    • Legislative Assembly: Union Territories with legislative assemblies have a degree of self-governance, with powers to make laws on certain subjects as defined by the Constitution and the relevant legislative framework.
  • Purpose and Impact:
    • Self-Governance: The article supports self-governance in Union Territories by allowing the establishment of legislative assemblies, providing a platform for local representation and decision-making.
    • Administrative Efficiency: Legislative assemblies in Union Territories can address local issues more effectively and tailor laws and policies to meet regional needs.
    • Enhanced Representation: By establishing legislative assemblies, the article ensures that the residents of Union Territories have a voice in their governance and administrative matters.
