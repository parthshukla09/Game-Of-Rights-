Article 243: Administration of Union Territories without Legislature

  • Governance Framework:
    • Scope: Article 243 deals with Union Territories that do not have legislative assemblies. For these Union Territories, the administration is managed directly by the President or through appointed administrators.
    • Administrative Control: The President has the authority to oversee the administration and governance of these Union Territories, ensuring that their legal and administrative needs are addressed.
  • Purpose and Impact:
    • Centralized Administration: The article supports a centralized approach to governance in Union Territories without legislative assemblies, ensuring consistent administration directly under the President.
    • Direct Control: The provision allows for direct control and management of Union Territories, which can be effective in maintaining order and implementing policies where local legislative bodies are not present.
    • Streamlined Administration: By not having a legislative assembly, these Union Territories can streamline administrative processes and ensure that governance is managed efficiently from a central authority.
